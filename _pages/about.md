<section id="research" class="profile-section research-page single-page-section" markdown="1">
<h2 id="research-title">Research</h2>

Lyman-continuum emission directly traces escaping ionizing radiation, making LyC galaxies key laboratories for understanding how galaxies contributed to cosmic reionization.

<div class="research-grid">
  <article class="research-topic">
    <h3>Physical properties</h3>
    <p>I study LyC leakers in GOODS-S and test whether LyC escape is connected to starburst activity, mergers, and disturbed morphology (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/ad7b18">Zhu et al. 2024</a>; <a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/adc125">Zhu et al. 2025</a>).</p>
  </article>
  <article class="research-topic">
    <h3>LCE searches</h3>
    <p>I worked on LCEz4-M1, a z = 4.444 Lyman-continuum emitter candidate reported as the highest-redshift example to date (<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01487">Zhu et al. 2026a</a>), and contributed to the search for CDFS-6664, a z~3.8 LyC leaker candidate (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/ac4170">Yuan et al. 2021</a>).</p>
  </article>
  <article class="research-topic">
    <h3>Data and methods</h3>
    <p>I combine HST and JWST imaging, photometry, SED fitting, and morphology measurements to interpret faint high-redshift galaxy candidates.</p>
  </article>
</div>

<section class="research-block" markdown="1">
### LyC Leakers at z > 3 in GOODS-S

In the GOODS-S field, I study the physical properties of LyC leakers selected from deep HST data. This work combines photometry, SED fitting, image morphology, and environmental information to test whether LyC escape is associated with starburst activity, merger signatures, or disturbed structures.
</section>

<section class="research-block" markdown="1">
### HDHα and HST Calibration Programs

I also work on space-telescope data products and calibration programs. For the Hubble Deep Hydrogen Alpha (HDHα) project, I completed HST narrowband deep-field reduction and catalog construction, supporting emission-line galaxy samples and deep-field survey science (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4365/ad148b">Zhu et al. 2024</a>). I also analyze HST calibration fields observed with ACS and WFC3, using their sky distribution and exposure depth to support calibration-field selection and data-analysis strategies for future space astronomy missions.
</section>

<section class="research-block research-block--tools" markdown="1">
### Methods and Tools

Image reduction and processing with Drizzlepac, SWarp, and PSF construction; photometry and catalog construction with SExtractor and Photutils; SED fitting with CIGALE and BAGPIPES; image decomposition with GALFIT; coding in Python, Bash, and LaTeX.
</section>
</section>
