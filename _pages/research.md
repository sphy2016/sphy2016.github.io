## Ph.D. Work on LyC Galaxies

My Ph.D. work follows two connected directions. First, I study the physical properties of LyC leakers in GOODS-S, testing whether LyC escape is connected to starburst activity, mergers, and disturbed morphology (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/ad7b18">Zhu et al. 2024</a>; <a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/adc125">Zhu et al. 2025</a>). Second, I search for high-redshift Lyman-continuum emitters; this includes LCEz4-M1, a z = 4.444 LCE candidate reported as the highest-redshift example to date (<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01487">Zhu et al. 2026a</a>), and my contribution to the search for CDFS-6664, a z~3.8 LyC leaker candidate (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/ac4170">Yuan et al. 2021</a>).

## HDHα and HST Calibration Programs

I also work on space-telescope data products and calibration programs. For the Hubble Deep Hydrogen Alpha (HDHα) project, I completed HST narrowband deep-field reduction and catalog construction, supporting emission-line galaxy samples and deep-field survey science (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4365/ad148b">Zhu et al. 2024</a>). I also analyze HST calibration fields observed with ACS and WFC3, using their sky distribution and exposure depth to support calibration-field selection and data-analysis strategies for future space astronomy missions.

This direction includes image stacking, calibration checks, emission-line selection, photometric catalog construction, and interpretation of space-telescope calibration fields and high-redshift galaxy samples.
